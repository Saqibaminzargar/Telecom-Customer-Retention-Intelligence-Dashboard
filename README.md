# Telecom-Customer-Retention-Intelligence-Dashboard
---
An end-to-end **Power BI** dashboard that analyzes customer churn behavior for a telecom company, uncovering *why* customers leave and *where* the business should focus to improve retention and protect revenue.
---
## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most expensive problems telecom companies face. This project analyzes a dataset of **7,043 customers** to identify churn drivers across demographics, tenure, services, and customer feedback turning raw data into an actionable retention strategy for the business.

The dashboard is built across **5 interactive pages**, each focused on a different lens on churn: overview, demographics, tenure, services, and churn reasons/revenue impact.
---
## Business Problem

The company wants to understand:
- Which customers are churning, and why
- Which cities and customer segments are highest-risk
- How service type and pricing relate to churn
- What the actual revenue impact of churn is
- Where retention efforts should be prioritized for maximum impact

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Summary / Overview
High-level KPIs, customer status breakdown (Stayed/Churned/Joined), churn rate gauge, and top churn cities.

### 2. Customer Demographics & Segmentation
Churn broken down by gender, marital status, age group, and revenue contribution across segments.

### 3. Tenure Analysis
Churn patterns across customer lifecycle stages from onboarding through long-term retention.

### 4. Services & Internet Type
Churn and revenue analyzed by internet service type, phone service, and monthly charges.

### 5. Churn Reasons & Revenue Impact
Root-cause breakdown of churn (competitor, dissatisfaction, price, attitude) and city-level revenue impact.

## Key Insights

- **San Diego and Los Angeles** drive the highest churn volumes — top priority cities for retention campaigns
- **Unmarried customers**, especially females, churn at a noticeably higher rate than married customers
- **Married customers are higher-value and more loyal**, contributing more revenue overall
- **Onboarding is the danger zone** — the highest churn occurs within the first 1–3 months of tenure
- Churn declines significantly the longer a customer stays, reinforcing the value of early retention efforts
- **Fiber Optic customers** generate the highest revenue but also churn the most, driven by higher monthly charges
- Customers with **Phone Service + Fiber Optic** show the highest churn rate of any service combination
- Nearly **half of all churn (45%)** is attributed to customers switching to competitors, followed by dissatisfaction (17%) and attitude (17%)
- Revenue is fairly balanced between male and female customers, and peaks in the **30–40 age group** before declining after 50

---

## Business Impact & Recommendations

- Prioritize retention campaigns in **San Diego and Los Angeles**
- Strengthen the **onboarding experience** (first 1–3 months) to reduce early churn
- Investigate **competitive pricing** on Fiber Optic plans, the largest revenue source and highest-churn segment
- Design targeted offers for **unmarried customers**, who show the highest churn propensity
- Address the **root causes of churn** — competitor offers and dissatisfaction through proactive retention outreach
